The most relevant classes I've taken are listed on my resume.

I am an undergraduate teaching assistant in Systems Fundamentals (COMP 211), an intermediate level computer science class. It teaches programming in the C language and lower-level computer concepts such as data representation, memory management, and parsing, as well as tools. With the help of Cindy Wang and Dylan Binley, I helped make an archival semester website.

I originally gained an appreciation for computer programming through Minecraft modding. Although I am no longer as active in the scene, I have created, taken over, or contributed towards several Minecraft mods used by many real players. Nuclear Control 2 (now discontinued) is probably my biggest project, which has about 5 million downloads. Notable other projects include the AFSU Mod (with 155k downloads), Flaxbeard's Steam Power (also known as Esteemed Innovation, with 935k downloads), World Control (981k downloads), Long Fall Boots (12.7m downloads), and HuesoDeWiki (with 4k downloads). All are written in Java.

Creating mods accidentally brought me deep into the world of documenting mods. I am now an administrator of and a major contributor (with over 50,000 edits) towards the Official FTB Wiki, the largest, most popular and most up-to-date centralized repository of knowledge on modded Minecraft. Because of classwork, I am only sporadically active, and my current contributions are mostly administrative rather than writing new documentation. In August 2016, I was awarded Gamepedia Editor of the Month for my "hard work across Gamepedia."
