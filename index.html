Hello! My name is Eric Schneider (he/him).

I was born in Charlotte, North Carolina, and raised in Weddington, North Carolina. I am currently an undergraduate college student at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I am a double major in Computer Science (BS) and Mathematics (BS). I am a sophomore.

Welcome to my website! This website is perpetually a work-in-progress, but it's mostly up-to-date. I am currently interested in summer internships as well as undergraduate research opportunities.

Coursework and experience

In computer science, my course load is focused around prerequisites, required preparation for the Master of Science, and classes that are generally very applicable (such as COMP 426: Modern Web Programming). In mathematics, my course load is focused around prerequisites. Once those are done, I am hoping to focus on scientific computation and mathematics relevant to the computer science field.

I am an undergraduate teaching assistant in Systems Fundamentals (COMP 211), an intermediate level computer science class. It teaches programming in the C language and lower-level computer concepts such as data representation, memory management, and parsing, as well as tools.

I originally gained an appreciation for computer programming through Minecraft modding. Although I am no longer especially active in the scene, I have created, taken over, or contributed towards several Minecraft mods used by many real players. Nuclear Control 2 (now discontinued) was probably my biggest project, with over 4 million downloads. Notable other projects include the AFSU Mod, Flaxbeard's Steam Power (also known as Esteemed Innovation), World Control, Long Fall Boots, and HuesoDeWiki. All are written in Java.

Creating mods accidentally brought me far into the world of documenting mods. I am now an administrator of and major contributor (with over 50,000 edits) towards the Official FTB Wiki, the largest, most popular and most up-to-date centralized repostiory of knowledge on modded Minecraft. Because of classwork, I am only sporadically active, and current contributions are mostly administrative rather than writing new documentation. In August 2016, I was awarded Gamepedia Editor of the Month for my "hard work across Gamepedia."

Technology expertise

Java is the programming language I feel most natural in. Groovy comes close, but only since they are quite similar. I'm not as skilled in Ruby, but I've always had a thing for it.
